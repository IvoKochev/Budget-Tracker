My Bills Questions

close view full page

Page Description

This page will allow you to save all your monthly bills. When creating a new bill you can add the name, amount, recurrence, and date of your monthly bill. The "Pay Selected Bills" button below will automatically enter transactions for the selected Bills using today's date and mark them as paid. The "pay bill" link will allow you to modify the date and amount before the Transaction is recorded. Additionally, in the monthly forecast you can click on the payment amount link to modify the amount of that single occurrence.
*Note: If the day you choose comes after the last day of the month, your bill will be shown as the last day of the month (example:Bill day=31 in February, your bill will now be the 28th or 29th).

Terms

Amortization Schedule
This will break down by month how long it will take with your interest rate to payoff a loan.
Transfer To Account
This will setup a transfer recurrence most commonly used when paying down a credit card.
Dynamically Set Budget
Checking this will automatically pull your budget amount for that category from this recurrence on the My Bills page.
Total Due
This will allow you to track how much is left due. Use this only if you are not setting up a transfer.
Payee
This is the name of the receiver of the payment you are making your bill payment to.
Amount
This is how much you plan to pay each month or recurrence. For the total due, you can create an account on My Accounts.

Questions & Answers

Why isn't my bill showing up in Monthly Forecast?
First check that you don't have a filter set at the top for your accounts. Then go to the Bills List and verify the start and end date you have set versus the month or custom date your are looking at in the Monthly Forecast.
How do I change the amount I want to record but leave my recurring amount the same?
You can do this by clicking the "record" link instead of using the checkbox. This will let you change all aspects of the transaction being created. If the amount is less than the scheduled amount, you'll see a link to mark as paid.
How do I setup a credit card?
First create your credit card account on the My Accounts page. Then create a recurring transfer from your checking account to your credit card. Enter in your interest to see an amortization of when it will get paid off.
how do you put in a recurring payment for an amount that may change each month?
So that we can give you a forecast and an estimated budget amount, we recommend entering an estimated amount at first. You can then change the amount on an individual basis each month by clicking the amount link.
I recorded the bill but it still says unpaid.
This usually happens if you enter the transaction manually instead of from the My Bills page. We attempt to tie manual entries when found but can sometimes get missed. To tie it to an existing transaction, hover over the bill name and choose "tie to existing transaction", then choose your transaction to tie it to.
I somehow hit "override" and it is now showing an "o" next to a bill every month. What is override and how do I turn it off?
It looks like you have an ongoing override which replaces your existing amount with a new amount. If you click on the amount link, then click "edit overrides", you should be able to delete it.
I have set up my accounts and credit cards and entered my credit card payment as a bill. I have applied payment to the bill (paid from my checking account) but my credit card balance did not decrease. It seems like my payment isn't tied to the credit card balance. Can you please walk me through this?
First make sure you bill is scheduled to transfer funds to your credit card by going to My Bills, click the transfer bill and making sure that "Transfer To" is chedked. Once you make the payment, go to the My Bills page and you can either check the checkbox next to your credit cards and then click the "Record Payment" button at the bottom, or if the amount is different than your estimate, click on the "record" link next to the bill so you can modify the amount before entering the payment.
is bills same as loans?
They're similar but the My Bills page is mainly used to track payments to your accounts whereas My Loans are accounts you owe money to for tracking. When you setup a loan bill schedule, we recommend you set it up as a transfer from your checking to your loan account so that you can see both the withdrawal portion and the deposit portion of the transaction and how your net income is effected.
what happens if i hit carryover on a bill i partially paid
If you mark it as paid it will carry over the partially paid amount, otherwise it will carry over the full amount.
is it best to use auto record?
If your bill happens on the day you choose and is usually the amount you've entered, then yes.
what are check boxes for
You should see 3 buttons at the bottom that will allow you to delete or record multiple bills at once
how do i pay bill
You can record bills as paid from the website by clicking the record link but you can't actually pay a bill from the site.
how do I show bill is paid
You can either click the record payment and enter a transaction for that bill or click the checkbox to the left of the bill and click the Record Payments button at the bottom. Alternatively, you can auto record your bills by editing the bill and choosing the auto record checkbox so that you don't need to enter the bill manually. This is useful when your bill date and amount rarely change.
What does auto record mean?
It mean that we will record the transaction for you on the date you enter so that you don't have to manually add it. This is use full for recurrences that are always a set amount.
Is there a way to view bills due by pay dates?
Yes, at the bottom of the My Bills page you should see a settings section. If you click the show payment date option, this will show the date the bill was paid next to the date the bill is due.
Is there a way that I can email my bills list
You can download them to a spreadsheet instead by clicking More... > Download Data and choosing the Bills List option.
